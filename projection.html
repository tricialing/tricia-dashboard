Government Incentives · First-Time Buyer · BC 2026
BC Property Transfer Tax Exemption
First-time buyers in BC are exempt from PTT on the first $500k of a home up to $835k. On a $565k purchase your PTT drops from ~$9,800 to ~$1,300. Must be Canadian citizen/PR and lived in BC 12+ consecutive months.
~$8,500 saved
First Home Savings Account (FHSA)
$8,000/year per person, tax-deductible contributions, tax-free withdrawal for home purchase. T has $3,057 already. Keep maxing — every dollar contributed also reduces taxable income. You and T combined = up to $16,000/year.
$3,057 + ongoing
RRSP Home Buyers' Plan (HBP)
Withdraw up to $60,000 per person from RRSP tax-free for a home purchase. T has $3,615 now — small but free. Repay over 15 years interest-free, starting year 5. Worth using at any amount.
$3,615 available
First-Time Home Buyers' Tax Credit
Claim a $10,000 non-refundable federal tax credit the year you buy. Translates to roughly $1,500 actual cash back on your tax return. Covers moving costs and first grocery haul.
~$1,500 back
BC Home Owner Grant
Annual property tax reduction of $570 for Metro Vancouver/Delta once you move in. Applies every year as long as it's your primary residence. Already factored into the $155/mo property tax estimate above.
$570/yr ongoing
30-Year Amortization
With 20%+ down you qualify for 30-year amortization without CMHC insurance. Keeps monthly payments lower through the school years. At $565k this saves ~$300/mo vs. a 25-year amortization.
~$300/mo saving
