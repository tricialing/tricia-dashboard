Government Incentives · First-Time Buyer · BC 2026
BC Property Transfer Tax Exemption
First-time buyers in BC are exempt from PTT on the first $500k of a home up to $835k. On a $565k purchase your PTT drops from ~$9,800 to ~$1,300. Must be Canadian citizen/PR and lived in BC 12+ consecutive months.
~$8,500 saved
First Home Savings Account (FHSA)
$8,000/year per person, tax-deductible contributions, tax-free withdrawal for home purchase. T has $3,057 already. Keep maxing — every dollar contributed also reduces taxable income. You and T combined = up to $16,000/year.
$3,057 + ongoing
RRSP Home Buyers' Plan (HBP)
Withdraw up to $60,000 per person from RRSP tax-free for a home purchase. T has $3,615 now — small but free. Repay over 15 years interest-free, starting year 5. Worth using at any amount.
$3,615 available
First-Time Home Buyers' Tax Credit
Claim a $10,000 non-refundable federal tax credit the year you buy. Translates to roughly $1,500 actual cash back on your tax return. Covers moving costs and first grocery haul.
~$1,500 back
BC Home Owner Grant
Annual property tax reduction of $570 for Metro Vancouver/Delta once you move in. Applies every year as long as it's your primary residence. Already factored into the $155/mo property tax estimate above.
$570/yr ongoing
30-Year Amortization
With 20%+ down you qualify for 30-year amortization without CMHC insurance. Keeps monthly payments lower through the school years. At $565k this saves ~$300/mo vs. a 25-year amortization.
~$300/mo saving
Total Day-1 Government Contribution ~$10,070+ upfront · $570/yr ongoing
Why Buying Now Is the Right Move

Every mortgage payment has two parts. Interest — the cost of borrowing, similar to rent. And principal — which is yours forever. From day one you're building something. Every rent payment disappears completely.

At $2,836/month you're not spending more than you do now — you're redirecting $2,950 that currently goes to your landlord into an asset you own. The difference is $114/month in your favour, plus equity compounding quietly in the background.

Vancouver/Delta real estate has weathered everything. Gulf War. 9/11. 2008 financial crisis. COVID. It dipped and recovered every time. Your horizon is 2030 — you're not selling in a panic, you're selling when you have your Dr.TCM designation and a clinic opening. That's a position of strength, not desperation.

You're also buying a home to live in, not just an investment. Stability for June. No landlord. Two bathrooms for a family of three. A storage room that becomes a TCM apothecary. Roots while you're doing the hardest academic work of your life. That has real value that doesn't show up in a spreadsheet.

By 2028 when R.Ac comes in, you'll have two incomes, ~$174k in equity already built, and savings stacking fast. The family home in 2030 doesn't require a miracle — it's just the next step in a plan that's already working.
