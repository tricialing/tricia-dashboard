Part One

The Life Philosophy

Where this began

Listening Season is my project to learn in public. While I prep for TCM school, while I go through it, and probably long after. It's built around the 24 solar terms because I found them interesting and I like the story. But the more I looked, the more I realised everything always connects back to TCM. The solar terms, the seasons, the way the body moves through the year. It's all the same system.

TCM culture was always around me growing up. It was just there, in the food, in the way people talked about health, in the kitchen. Always simple on the surface but somehow too big to fully explain. I was always curious but never had a real reason to go deep into it. Until now. Now I realise I'm going to be the one using these skills on my own family. That changes things.

The thing that shifted something fundamental in me was my birth experience. We went with a physiological home water birth. It was magical. It was painful. It changed me completely. My doula kept reminding me throughout: the only way out is through. Trust your body to do what it was built to do. When we finally got to meet our child, I had never felt more empowered or strong. It was a pivotal moment in understanding my own power.

That experience gave me a new perspective on what the body is capable of when you actually listen to it and work with it. Pregnancy, healing, breastfeeding, becoming a safe place for a little person while trying to stay a whole person. All of it deepened my curiosity for the body and the system around it. How it works, what it knows, what it is trying to tell you when you slow down enough to hear it.

I believe your body holds the answer when you listen to it.

What I believe

The body is intelligent
It is always communicating. Most of modern life is designed to help you not hear it. Listening is a practice, not a talent.
Slowness is not laziness
Moving at the speed of the seasons, not the speed of productivity culture, is a form of precision. Things done in season last.
The complete picture matters
Quick symptom suppression answers the wrong question. TCM, bodywork, seasonal living. These are attempts to ask better questions.
Design is a lifestyle
The way you arrange your time, your home, your attention, your food. These are not aesthetic choices. They are how you live.
You learn by doing publicly
The living notebook. The study in public. The honest documentation of being in the middle of something, not on the other side of it.
The body holds the family
To care for yourself seasonally is to become someone your family can lean on. Not because you are unbreakable, but because you are paying attention.

The living principles

These are not rules. They are orientations. Directions to face when lost.

01 · Listen before it screams.
The body gives small signals long before it gives loud ones. The practice is catching them early. The slight fatigue, the jaw tension, the craving for quiet.

02 · Move with the season, not against it.
Energy contracts in winter and expands in summer for a reason. Working against this costs more than it earns.

03 · Information over comfort.
The full picture, even when it's uncomfortable, is more useful than a managed version that feels fine.

04 · Progress over perfection.
The draft, the click, the work in progress. These are not failures of completion. They are the documentation of becoming.

05 · Returning, not consistency.
Not the pressure of never missing a day. The practice of always coming back.

06 · Design your life before life designs you.
Intentional arrangement of time, attention, and space. Or the default will arrange it instead.

Who I am making this for

Not a demographic. A specific person.

She's curious. Probably a bit obsessive about understanding things once she gets interested in them. She's likely in a season of life where the body is asking more of her attention than it used to. Motherhood, a big transition, something that made her stop and actually look. She doesn't want a quick fix or a morning routine to copy. She wants to understand the system.

She grew up with this culture somewhere in the background too. Maybe TCM, maybe just the feeling that there was more to health than what she'd been told. She never fully followed the thread. And now something is making her want to.

She is where I was. The work I'm doing publicly is the map I wish I'd had. And honestly, I'm still drawing it.

Part Two

The Brand Blueprint

What Listening Season is

A public living notebook. A documentation of one person learning to move through time differently. Through the framework of the 24 solar terms, through the study and eventual practice of TCM, through the ordinary dailiness of motherhood and a body being paid attention to.

Not a finished thing. Not a course or a programme. An ongoing act of noticing, done in public, that accumulates into something useful for others who are doing the same.

Mission

To document, honestly and in public, the practice of listening to the body and the seasons before they have to scream. And to make that practice legible to people who have been living too fast for too long.

The voice

Who I am
A fellow traveller. A mother. A student. Someone in the middle of learning this, not on the other side. The woman in the mud, not the guide on the hill.
Tone
Warm but not soft. Honest but not confessional. Curious before prescriptive. The friend who has done the research and wants to share it, not the expert who already has the answers.
What I never am
A guru. An influencer performing wellness. Someone who has it figured out. Someone selling a solution to a problem I claim not to have.

Aesthetic direction

"Structural Organic." The intersection of a designer's precision and a gardener's hands.

Visual: High-grain film photography. Natural side-lighting. Macro shots of nature in Delta, BC.

Typography: Serif, widely spaced, lowercase for the softer words. Chinese characters small. Atmosphere, not headline.

Colour: Your 24 hex codes. The year as a gradient from blush spring to cool winter.

Feeling: Soft but not cute. Warm but not lazy. Inviting but with clear boundaries.

Content strategy — the 15-day pulse

Every 15 days the heartbeat shifts with the solar term. Three phases within each cycle:

The Threshold
Day 1
Announce the new term. Define its energy. Set the tone for the next two weeks.
The Immersion
Days 2–10
Listening out loud. Work from Alive Field Notes. Rotate through the 6 streams: Nature, Body, Mind, Food, Care, Story.
The Foreshadowing
Days 11–15
Notice the bleed into the next term. Let the transition be visible. Prepare the community for the shift.

The 6 content streams

Nature
Local observations from Delta, BC. What the season looks and sounds and smells like here, now.
Body
Physical sensations, bodywork, maintenance, what the body is doing and asking for.
Mind
Emotional reflections. Being a mother and a human and a student. The interior landscape.
Food
Seasonal recipes, nourishment, process cooking. Food as medicine without the dogma.
Care
Rituals, tending, the small acts of sustaining a life.
Story
Myth, personal narrative, the arc of the season and the year.

Community architecture

We are not building a following. We are gathering Fellow Travellers.

Instagram
The front door. Visual, atmospheric, the 15-day pulse made visible.
Newsletter
The inner circle. Deeper notes, seasonal reflections, early access. Moves over time to a paid tier.
Printables & zines
The passive income layer. Observation Logs (free lead magnet), Solar Terms Planner (paid), seasonal zines. Physical objects that carry the philosophy.
The clinic (2030)
The whole arc lands here. The brand audience becomes the warm referral network. Listening Season and the practice are the same thing.

What this is not

Written here as protection, for the moments when comparison or drift makes it tempting to become something else.

– Not a productivity system dressed up as wellness.

– Not a personal brand that performs having it together.

– Not a TCM marketing platform that uses the solar terms as content hooks.

– Not a hustle. Not a grind. Not consistent at the expense of true.

– Not finished. Ever.

The living notebook principle

This project is designed to grow in layers, not to be replaced. The notes from 2026, the beginner's eye, the basic observations, the first season of paying attention, are part of the archive. In 2028 the notes will be richer. In 2030 they will carry clinical depth. The same 24 terms, revisited every year. The accumulation is the point.

You never delete old notes. You add layers. The person you were at the beginning is part of the story.
