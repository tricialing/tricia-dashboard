Apple Reminders Setup
Task tracking for an ADHD brain that already uses Apple
10 minutes to set up. Then just use it.

Why this works for you

Already on your phone, your Mac, your iPad. Syncs automatically via iCloud. Works with Siri — hands full with June, voice command. No new app to learn, no subscription, no setup rabbit hole.

The ADHD brain needs the external list because working memory is unreliable — not because you're disorganised, but because your brain processes everything at once and things leak. Reminders is the container that catches what leaks.

The rule: if it needs to happen, it goes in Reminders. If it needs to happen at a specific time, it also goes in Google Cal. Those are the only two places.

Step 1 — Create your four lists

Open Reminders → tap Add List in the bottom left. Create these four, in this order:

📚
School
Assignments, readings, exam prep, clinical hours
🌱
Listening Season
Content, products, doc updates, brand tasks
🏠
Family
June, T, household, appointments
⚙️
Admin
Condo, finances, life logistics

Leave the default Reminders list as your Inbox. Everything lands here first. You sort it later — never in the moment of capture.

Step 2 — Set up your recurring tasks

These go in now so they're already there when you need them. In each list, create these:

In Listening Season

📋 Review living docs — every 15 days (align with solar term start)
philosophy.html, brand-voice.html, mindmap.html — does anything need updating?

📸 Threshold post — every 15 days (same day as doc review)
New solar term begins. Instagram post + start new Observation Log.

📬 LS newsletter — once per solar term, day 7 or 8
Mid-term reflection. No pressure on length.

In Admin

🗂 Inbox sort — every Tuesday and Friday, 15 mins
Move items from Reminders inbox into the right list. Nothing else.

📅 Weekly preview — every Sunday evening
Open Reminders + Google Cal together. What's coming this week? Any prep needed?

Step 3 — The one discipline

Capture everything. Sort twice a week. Never try to do both at the same time.

When something crosses your mind — a task, a thing to follow up, something you don't want to forget — it goes into the Reminders inbox immediately. One tap, one line, no filing. The sorting happens Tuesday and Friday in 15 minutes.

This works with ADHD because it separates two different brain states: the capture state (fast, low friction, any time) and the sort state (slow, deliberate, scheduled). Trying to file perfectly in the moment is where systems collapse.

Step 4 — Use Siri for capture

The fastest capture is voice, especially when you're with June. Siri adds directly to Reminders inbox.

Say to Siri
"Hey Siri, remind me to update the brand voice doc"
"Hey Siri, add email chiropractor to my reminders"
"Hey Siri, remind me tomorrow at 9am to check the strata notice"
"Hey Siri, add buy sourdough starter to reminders"

It all lands in the inbox. You sort it Tuesday and Friday. Nothing falls through.

When school starts — September 8

The School list becomes your most active list. For each semester, the pattern is:

Week 1
Add all assignment due dates and exam dates to both Reminders (School list) and Google Cal immediately. Set a reminder 3 days before each one.
Readings
One task per reading. "Read Chapter 4 — Liver & Gallbladder." When done, check it off. Small completions matter for the ADHD brain.
Exams
Break exam prep into subtasks in Reminders. Not "study for exam" — "review Lecture 3 notes", "make flashcards for meridian points", "practice case study questions."
Clinical hours
Log in Google Cal as you complete them. Keep a running total in Capacities. You will need this record.

Your full stack — one more time

Google Cal
Time. Appointments, deadlines, reminders with alerts. If it has a date and time, it lives here.
Apple Reminders
Tasks. Everything that needs to happen but isn't time-specific. Inbox → sort twice a week.
Capacities
Notes and memory. Study, daily record, observations, TLDR at the end of the day.
WhatsApp pipe
Capture in the moment → Capacities. One sentence, right now, no formatting.
Pen & paper
Morning 3 things. The daily grounding. Don't digitise this.
Dashboard
Orientation. The big picture. Where you are, what season it is, what matters.

Five tools. Each with one job. Nothing overlaps. When something feels lost, ask: which of these five should it live in?

When to graduate to Things 3

Apple Reminders will carry you through to at least semester 3 or 4. The moment to reconsider is when you have:

– Multiple concurrent projects with subtasks that depend on each other

– Clinical hours tracking that needs more structure

– Brand projects with deadlines, deliverables, and collaborators

– The feeling that Reminders is too flat and you keep losing threads

That moment is probably 2028. Until then — don't fix what isn't broken.
