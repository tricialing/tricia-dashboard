Mar 12 2026 · 驚蟄 · Stream: Dashboard / Systems / Series material Continued from the series conversation. The backstory behind the dashboard -- years of research before Claude was part of it. The dashboard didn't come out of nowhere. There is a bigger history behind all of this before my system is this. It is from years of research. Systems I have studied and applied to my life: Morning Pages (Julia Cameron) -- this is the ONE that got me used to writing and writing and flowing. It came before everything else. It gave me permission to just let things out without them being good yet. GTD (Getting Things Done) -- learned to externalize everything, get it out of my head. Bullet Journal (Ryder Carroll) -- learned to track in my own hand, my own system. He built it for his own ADHD brain because nothing else worked. He shared it because it felt wrong not to. Building a Second Brain / Zettelkasten / How to Take Smart Notes -- learned that captured notes are raw material, not finished thoughts. Currently reading How to Take Smart Notes and applying it here: capture first, then review and connect, then assemble. Claude -- finally had something that could hold all of it with me and help me move from capture to output. Each one taught me something real. None of them fully worked alone. Claude didn't replace the research. It finally gave me a way to implement what I had already learned. The pattern I keep finding: I have always captured. But I don't review or create. More precisely -- I create, but I don't finalize until things feel like complete thoughts. So a lot of the same thoughts just loop around in my head without developing further. The habit to build is not capture. It is release. Letting something out before it feels fully formed. The thought develops further in public, not in your head. -- This is a missing chapter of the arc for Help Needed. Sits between "the gap" and "I just wanted to try." The research years.

Body & Health
What does taking care of your body actually look like for you right now?
Swimming. Getting back into it slowly. It's the one thing that feels good in my body without hurting it. Tonal — the strength training at home. I like that it's quiet and I can do it on my terms. The chiro who does tonal adjustments, not the crack-your-back kind. That felt right in a way I didn't expect. Acupuncture. Mostly for the jaw. It's been years of tension there. My wrists too. I don't know if I'm holding something or if it's just the way I work. The somatic stuff is new. I booked the first session because the jaw and wrist tension kept coming back and I started wondering if there was something under it.
What are you most curious about when it comes to your body?
TCM. Obviously. But more specifically — the idea that the body keeps a kind of record. That the jaw clenching is not random, that the wrist tension is not just from typing. That there's a logic to it even if I can't read it yet. I want to learn to read it. That's why I'm going to school.
Motherhood
What has surprised you most about being June's mother?
How much I enjoy watching her learn things. Like genuinely enjoy it, not in a performed way. The way she figures out how a thing works. I see myself in that. Also how hard it is to have her home on the days I need to work. Not because I don't want her there. Just — the switching. The context switching is brutal for my brain.
What do you want for her that you didn't have?
I want her to know her own rhythms earlier than I did. To not spend her thirties figuring out what her twenties were actually about. I want her to feel like her brain is an asset, not a liability.
Creative Work
What does creative work feel like when it's going well?
Like I'm not doing it. Like it's already there and I'm just finding it. That sounds pretentious but it's the most accurate thing I can say. When the design or the writing is right, it doesn't feel like effort. It feels like recognition. The dashboard is like that. Each page — when it comes together — it feels like: yes, that's it. That's what was already in my head.
What gets in the way?
The gap between what I can see and what I can make. And the maintenance. I can start things. I cannot maintain things without a system that does it with me. That's what's different now. The dashboard doesn't require me to maintain it alone.
Learning & School
Why TCM? The real answer.
Because I've been receiving it for years and it kept working when other things didn't. Because I want to understand the system, not just benefit from it. Because I think there's something in the way it reads the body — as a whole, over time, in relationship to seasons and cycles — that feels true in a way that compartmentalised Western medicine often doesn't. Also because I want a second income that comes from my own hands and my own attention. That feels important.
What are you most afraid of about going back to school?
That I'll start and not finish. That the ADHD will catch up to me in a structured environment in a way it hasn't caught up to me in self-directed work. But I've been preparing differently this time. The anatomy prep. The accommodations application. I'm not walking in cold.
Home & Space
What do you want from a home?
It just seems so unreachable. Now that we are here our family dynamic has changed with my parents. Somehow we can talk about money more and I feel comfortable enough to ask them to help us buy. So that's interesting. With my interior design training I want to live in a place with some character and an ease of flow. Airy and bright. A functional large kitchen is welcomed. And I would like to have my own room again. Like my own study/craft room that is just all my stuff. I don't know about decorating. But maybe some art on the wall would be nice. Good storage and a good closet set up. I want to have a dedicated home within the home for all our stuff. I guess I want another system. Our last 3 moves were: first landing in Canada living with my parents in their spare bedroom and bath — never had time to open up our stuff. Then we moved out when I was 36 weeks pregnant so we never properly set up or bought furniture before we moved again when June was 6 months old. Then we moved to our current townhouse where we finally bought furniture and opened all the boxes. But I think we need more shelves or something. Things are still in piles. 2 years in I still don't feel like we are properly set up. And we are talking about moving AGAIN.
