Body & Health
Beyond TCM school and the birth experience — what is your actual relationship with your body right now? What do you notice, what do you ignore, what are you still figuring out?
I do yoga as my workout when I have a chance. But my preferred workout is swimming. I recently started swimming alone at the pool when June is at day care. It brings me so much peace and joy. I love that it is a no distraction activity. No music no podcast no phone. Just me and my breath. I often hurt myself from over working. Carpal tunnel, repetitive stress disorder for my fingers, TMJD. I recently started therapy cause I wanted to understand the signs for stress before I hurt myself. I learnt about stress cycle and that helped me catch myself being in stress cycle and regulate myself before I start the next thing. But I think my recent ADHD diagnosis is the most helpful on helping me figure out my needs and how to help myself work. And to explain my chronic pain and energy dip. I have been getting so much body work since getting pregnant. I have tried it all. My favorite of all is my tonal chiropractor who is able to help me with just her listening hands. I want to gain that ability. I did recently get trained/attuned for reiki because I wanted the listening hands and also my acupuncturist said it is helpful to understand the qi before starting acupuncture school.
Motherhood
Not the logistics, not the milestones. What has becoming June's mother changed about how you see yourself? And is there anything about motherhood that nobody talks about that you think about a lot?
Turns out I have so much more patience than I knew I have. I have almost never gotten mad at her. I am capable to give her (and myself) so much more grace than I knew possible. Watching her learn and figure things out has been the most fascinating experience. From rolling over to now learning to read. It is just so fulfilling. People always ask me if she is a good/easy baby. I always say that she is a fitting baby for our family. We co sleep and still cosleeping at 2.5, contact every single nap (until day care started), we are still breastfeeding. We are one. So she works well with our family but if she grew up in a different family it might be hard for that family to meet her needs. But she fit right into our family. We love being at home. We also love going on our version of adventures. She is always game to do whatever. It's so fun to be a mom. No regrets.
Creative Work
What does creativity mean to you outside of the work? Is there making you do just for yourself that has nothing to do with output or audience?
I have always wanted to paint. But I can't seem to let go and just blob colors on the canvas. I am either too cheap feeling like I'm wasting or I am scared. I like rules and structures. I blame my education in Hong Kong when I was a young child. But I just can't seem to break out from it. I started seriously making sourdough bread around 1.5 years ago. And now it has finally become second nature to me. I can read the dough, I don't need the timer for stretch and fold or proofing any more. I just feel it and touch it and it feels freeing. There is a set structure (weighing the material) but everything else is pretty loose. It is fun and freeing. I also read a fair bit. I go through waves of reading. This year I'm behind on my reading goal. My goal for the year is 36 and I have only read 1. I also knit. It is pretty second nature to me. I have always dreamed of making clothes for my child and myself. I had the image of sewing but I have knitted my child some items and she wears them and she knows that I made them. It feels good. Again. It is a very structured hobby. I still want to get into sewing but the set up and prep is too overwhelming. I never really got into it. I cook. I like to read cook books and recipes. I feel like I am in a flow when I cook even tho I might make a huge mess (ADHD) but I enjoy it.
Learning & School
What is your actual relationship with learning? What do you love about it, what's hard, what does school mean to you after all this time away from it?
I love school. I love seeing the syllabus and knowing what I HAVE to do to reach goals. Now I know that it is lists that help me work well. I don't find school and learning hard. I have a pretty good memory. But I want a better noting system because I never have one that's stable. I want to be those people who have piles of notebooks. Sure it sounds like I am trying to archive other people's life but when I keep a journal or a diary I reach conclusions that I didn't know was inside me. That's why I wanted to start the newsletter long form. I want to explore. I really look forward to being in school again. The completing the list and learning with a structure. But this time I have a greater grander purpose. I want to be useful to my family and hopefully to the world. I did a weekend of postpartum doula training when June was 8 months old thinking I could be a postpartum doula. But I am unwilling to put my child aside for other people's child. So I didn't follow down that path. But I think with acupuncture it will be a more realistic option to be a working mom and a stay at home mom at the same time especially where June will be in school more. I did think that I was gonna home school June for preschool and kindergarten but since I'm starting school that won't work. We did end up finding her a daycare that's Reggio based and focuses on going outside and nature 3 hours a day. She is so happy there. I am ok with this trade off. And I feel like I can still do homeschool lessons as play with her on off days.
Home & Space
What is your relationship with home — not the house hunting, the feeling of it?
When we were living in Hong Kong I never imagined myself buying a place. It just seems so unreachable. Now that we are here our family dynamic has changed with my parents. Somehow we can talk about money more and I feel comfortable enough to ask them to help us buy. So that's interesting. With my interior design training I want to live in a place with some character and an ease of flow. Airy and bright. A functional large kitchen is welcomed. And I would like to have my own room again. Like my own study/craft room that is just all my stuff. I don't know about decorating. But maybe some art on the wall would be nice. Good storage and a good closet set up. I want to have a dedicated home within the home for all our stuff. I guess I want another system. Our last 3 moves were: first landing in Canada living with my parents in their spare bedroom and bath — never had time to open up our stuff. Then we moved out when I was 36 weeks pregnant so we never properly set up or bought furniture before we moved again when June was 6 months old. Then we moved to our current townhouse where we finally bought furniture and opened all the boxes. But I think we need more shelves or something. Things are still in piles. 2 years in I still don't feel like we are properly set up. And we are talking about moving AGAIN.
