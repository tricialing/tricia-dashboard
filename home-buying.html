Home Buying
Be ready. Not desperate. The right place will show up.
A reference document — not a to-do list. Use this to think through what matters before you move. Actual tasks live in Reminders. Numbers live in projection.html.
The position you are building toward
~$128k down payment · 23%+ · no CMHC · mortgage less than current rent · parents can top up for closing · crypto as reserve. You are not stretching. You are ready.
Get financially ready — regardless of any specific property
Book a mortgage broker appointment
Free, no commitment. T needs to be there — it's his income being assessed. Goal: confirm T qualifies for ~$420–450k on $95k gross.
Get mortgage pre-approval
Once broker confirms qualification. Pre-approval is valid ~90–120 days and locks in a rate. Makes any offer stronger.
Confirm the parents' gift letter process with the broker
$100k gifted down payment needs a signed letter stating it's a gift not a loan. Broker will provide the template.
Max FHSA contribution this year
Annual limit is $8,000. Every dollar contributed is tax-deductible and grows tax-free toward the purchase.
Confirm RRSP Home Buyers' Plan eligibility with broker
Can withdraw tax-free, repay over 15 years. Confirm it can be combined with FHSA withdrawal in the same purchase.
Gather documents for pre-approval
T's last 2 NOAs, 2 recent pay stubs, employment letter, bank statements showing down payment funds.
Find a real estate agent who knows Ladner and Delta
Ask for referrals. Look for someone who knows strata buildings specifically. Agent fees are paid by the seller.
When you find the one — questions to ask before offering
Request strata minutes — last 2 years minimum
Look for recurring complaints, water damage, special levy discussions. Red flags = walk away.
Get the depreciation report
Check the contingency reserve fund health. Any major capital expenses coming? Is it adequately funded?
Ask why it has been sitting on the market
Days on market is leverage but also a question. Motivated seller or a hidden issue? Your agent should find out.
Book a home inspection
Make it a condition of the offer. T can review the building specs as an architect — he will catch things an inspector might miss.
Check for upcoming special levies
Ask the strata manager directly. Any levies passed or proposed? You are responsible from possession date.
Confirm pet bylaws in writing
Two cats. Get it confirmed in the strata bylaws, not verbally. BC law allows pets but stratas can set limits.
Offer and closing — things to know
Make the offer subject to financing and inspection
On a property sitting 90+ days, offer 3–5% below asking with confidence. Give 24–48hrs to respond.
Hire a real estate lawyer
Budget ~$1,500–2,000. They handle title transfer, strata documents, and closing paperwork. Required in BC.
Claim the BC PTT first-time buyer exemption
Saves ~$8,500 on a $565k purchase. Your lawyer handles this at closing — just make sure they know you qualify.
Apply for the BC Home Owner Grant
$570/year off property taxes. Apply after possession, before the annual deadline.
Claim the First-Time Home Buyers' Tax Credit on your tax return
$10,000 federal tax credit = ~$1,500 actual cash back. Claim it on the return for the year you buy.
