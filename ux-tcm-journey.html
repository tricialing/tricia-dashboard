The Patient Journey
From first finding you online to the long-term relationship
First draft March 2026. To be deepened as clinical training begins.
The Project Brief
This is a full UX overhaul of the TCM clinic experience, online and in person. The lighting, the sound, the atmosphere. It should feel good and look beautiful. A total experience, not just a treatment.
The Problem
We walk in and get asked "how are you feeling?" but we were never told what to notice. So it's "here hurts, I'm tired, I can't sleep." The practitioner asks questions but we weren't taught to observe, so how would we remember what our digestion looked like that week, or what time we were waking up at night? We weren't prepared. We arrive without the language.

Then the session happens. Sometimes at the end the practitioner tells you what they worked on and gives some tips. But nobody explains why you got here or how the pattern developed. You leave having been treated but not taught. The relationship stays transactional when it could be something else entirely.
What I want instead
I want patients to walk out knowing something about their body they didn't know before. To be able to read the signs. To understand what happened in the session, in their own words. Maybe they leave with a small take-home, the Observation Log for before the next appointment and a note on what we worked on today.

I want the whole experience to feel considered. The space, the intake process, the follow-up. Online and in person. Every touchpoint designed, not accumulated.
Why this is possible
A design background plus a TCM practice is an unusual combination. Most practitioners were never taught to think about the patient experience as a designed system. Most designers don't have clinical knowledge. The Listening Season audience, the Observation Log, the body literacy work starting in 2026, all of it feeds directly into who walks through the clinic door in 2030. The brand and the practice are the same project.
Stage 01 Discovery They find you
What they're feeling
  • Something is off but they can't name it
  • Tired of being told everything is fine
  • Curious about TCM but intimidated
  • Not sure if it's "for them"
  • Possibly postpartum, exhausted, unheard
Current pain points
  • TCM looks esoteric or inaccessible online
  • Can't tell if practitioner is trustworthy
  • Pricing is unclear
  • Don't know what a session even involves
  • Website looks like it was built in 2008
The LS vision
  • Instagram feels warm, specific, honest
  • She sounds like someone who gets it
  • Content teaches before asking anything
  • Solar terms make TCM feel liveable
  • She's clearly still learning, that's safe
The LS advantage: By the time someone books, they've been reading your content for months. They already trust you. The discovery phase is doing the relationship-building work that most clinics have to do in the first appointment.
Stage 02 Trust Building They follow, read, use the Observation Log
What they're feeling
  • Starting to recognise themselves in the content
  • Learning to notice their body for the first time
  • The Observation Log makes them feel capable
  • Something is shifting even before booking
Current pain points
  • Most practitioners have no content presence
  • No way to "try before you book"
  • No body literacy preparation for patients
  • Trust has to be built entirely in the room
The LS vision
  • Observation Log = free, useful, no pressure
  • Newsletter deepens the relationship over time
  • Zines make TCM tangible and beautiful
  • Seasonal workshops let them meet you first
Key insight: The Observation Log is pre-clinical training. A patient who has been using it for 6 months walks into their first appointment already knowing what their Liver does in spring, already tracking their energy and sleep patterns. That changes the entire session.
Stage 03 First Contact They decide to book
What they're feeling
  • Nervous, is this the right decision?
  • Uncertain about what to expect
  • Hoping it won't feel clinical or cold
  • Wondering if they'll be taken seriously
Current pain points
  • Booking systems are clunky or phone-only
  • No indication of what to prepare
  • Confirmation email is generic and cold
  • Long gap between booking and appointment
  • Nothing to do in the waiting period
The LS vision
  • Simple online booking, immediate confirmation
  • Warm welcome email, here's what to expect
  • Pre-intake sent with the Observation Log
  • Invitation to notice their body before arrival
  • No paper forms on the day
The pre-intake: Instead of a form they fill in on a clipboard in the waiting room, they receive a digital intake 3–5 days before their appointment. Calm, unhurried, in their own space. Includes the Observation Log as the body awareness warm-up.
Stage 04 The Intake Before they walk in
What they're feeling
  • Hopeful but unsure what will be asked
  • Possibly embarrassed they don't know their body well
  • Wanting to be helpful, not knowing how
Current pain points
  • "How are you feeling?" → "Fine, I guess"
  • Patient has never been taught to observe
  • Intake form asks yes/no, not open observation
  • Practitioner has to spend half the session on history
  • Patient forgets what they wanted to say
The LS vision
  • Pre-intake trains observation, not just history
  • Questions shaped around TCM patterns
  • Sleep, digestion, energy, mood, cravings, temperature
  • Seasonally framed, what has this term been like?
  • Practitioner arrives already knowing the landscape
The body literacy loop closes here: The Observation Log they've been using becomes their intake tool. They already have the language. "My energy has been a 2 in the mornings and a 4 by afternoon." "Strong cravings for sour things. Jaw tension. Vivid dreams." That's a TCM intake. You built the tool in 2026. They arrive in 2030 already fluent.
Stage 05 Appointment Day They walk in
What they're feeling
  • Vulnerable, letting someone work on their body
  • Hoping to feel heard, not processed
  • Possibly bringing a child, exhausted, rushed
  • Aware that their time is limited and precious
Current pain points
  • Waiting room is clinical, not welcoming
  • Running late with no communication
  • Practitioner reads notes while patient sits there
  • Session feels rushed, too much to cover
  • Family not considered, nowhere for a child to be
  • Left without understanding what just happened
The LS vision
  • Space feels like a considered home, not a clinic
  • Practitioner has read intake before they arrive
  • Family-friendly, the chiropractor model
  • Session starts with "what did you notice?" not "what's wrong?"
  • Patient leaves understanding the pattern, not just the treatment
  • Seasonal framing throughout, this is what Wood does in spring
The language shift: "What's wrong?" positions the patient as broken. "What did you notice?" positions them as the expert on their own body. That single question change is the whole UX TCM philosophy in practice.
Stage 06 Post-Appointment They leave
What they're feeling
  • Relieved, possibly emotional
  • Trying to remember what was said
  • Unsure what to do next
  • Uncertain when to rebook
Current pain points
  • No written summary of what was treated
  • Home care advice given verbally, forgotten by dinner
  • No check-in between appointments
  • Rebooking is the patient's responsibility to remember
  • Relationship resets to zero at next visit
The LS vision
  • Post-appointment note sent same day, what we worked on, what to notice
  • Home care in writing, food, movement, rest, what to watch for
  • 3-day check-in: "how are you feeling since Tuesday?"
  • Observation Log for the current solar term attached
  • Rebooking suggested with seasonal framing, spring liver work continues
The 3-day check-in: A single message, 3 days after the appointment. Not a form. Not a survey. Just: "How has your body been since Tuesday? Anything you've noticed?" That message is the difference between a transaction and a relationship. Almost no one does it.
Stage 07 The Ongoing Relationship The long arc
What they're feeling
  • Starting to trust their own body more
  • Coming back because it works, not just when crisis hits
  • Part of a community of people paying attention
  • Referring people they love
Current pain points
  • Most patients only come when something hurts
  • No seasonal rhythm to care
  • Practitioner relationship stays clinical, not relational
  • No reason to stay connected between appointments
The LS vision
  • Seasonal check-in at each solar term transition
  • Newsletter keeps the relationship warm between visits
  • Zines arrive in the mail, the practice comes to their home
  • Family treated as a unit, everyone has a relationship with the clinic
  • Body literacy grows year over year
  • They become the person who refers everyone they love
The flywheel: Listening Season builds the audience → the audience becomes patients → patients become the community → the community refers new patients → new patients find Listening Season. The brand and the clinic feed each other indefinitely.
